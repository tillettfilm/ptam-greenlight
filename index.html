Act 04 · The Film

Built for the stand. Made to travel further.

This film is designed first for Farnborough: a silent, looping 16:9 film displayed on a 42-inch screen above the stand. Its job is to draw the right people in, communicate PTAM's capability, precision and excellence, and start the right conversation before anyone has spoken to the specialist team.

01 · The Screen
Stop the right people.
The film needs to work at distance without relying on sound. Fast enough to catch attention and controlled enough to feel serious. Modern transitions, a strong visual rhythm and clear capability cues designed to spark serious commercial interest.
02 · The Message
Scale. Capability. Precision.
This is not about speed for the sake of speed. The film needs to show PTAM as a partner capable of handling large components, complex materials, repeatable quality and demanding aerospace and defence requirements.
03 · The Proof
A reactive business that delivers.
The visual story should show PTAM's ability to adapt quickly, think around customer needs and deliver with reliability. Machinery active. Materials handled carefully. Teams moving with purpose. Quality controlled. Confidentiality protected.

The film should say: PTAM has the scale, precision and trust to support serious aerospace and defence work without needing to over-explain it.Suitable for Farnborough, LinkedIn, the website, future shows and direct outreach. 4K · 16:9 · NDA-safe.

Act 06 · The Lookbook

How it will look. How it will feel.

Working Title
"Capability Made Visible."
Visual
Shot on cinema glass to give the manufacturing process texture and depth. The focus is the work. Materials being handled, components being shaped, machinery active, inspection, finishing, scale, precision and controlled movement. Office moments can support the story but the centre of the film is PTAM's capability.
Pace
Precise, modern and purposeful. Fast enough to hold attention on a 42 inch stand screen but controlled enough to feel credible to aerospace and defence buyers. The rhythm should come from the process itself: cutting, handling, checking, finishing and moving.
Sound
The film must work without sound at Farnborough. For LinkedIn, website and future shows, the sound design should be built from process: machinery, room tone, tools, material movement and subtle industrial texture. Mechanical and premium. Not a generic corporate track.
Structure
The opening establishes PTAM's advanced materials capability. The middle shows the process: machinery, people, materials, inspection and quality control. The close leaves one message: PTAM can handle serious aerospace and defence requirements with trust, control and repeatable quality.

How we make it.

From green light to final delivery. Final assets prepared ahead of Farnborough.

Green Light
Project Signed Off

Planning and Preparation
Shot list built, locations confirmed, NDA safe areas agreed, non-sensitive components identified, camera approach planned and equipment set for the production.
Shoot Day
